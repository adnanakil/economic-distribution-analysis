The Ingredients of Democratic Collapse
What conditions create fertile ground for extremist movements? Historical analysis from the 1920s to today reveals a consistent pattern: extremism rarely arises from a single cause. Instead, four key ingredients combine to create conditions for democratic backsliding.
The four ingredients that precede populist/fascist turns:
1. Economic Crisis (~33% of effect): Financial crises trigger unemployment, debt, uncertainty ‚Üí risk-seeking behavior. Meta-analysis of 36 studies confirms causal effect.

2. Status Threat (major driver): Perceived loss of national standing or group dominance. Stronger predictor than economics in 2016 US election.

3. Cultural Backlash (major driver): Demographic change, immigration ‚Üí anxiety and scapegoating. Cultural concerns can drive economic discontent (reverse causation).

4. Institutional Weakness (accelerant): Young democracies, mainstream party failure ‚Üí trust collapse.

The pattern: Weimar Germany, Greece 2009-2015, Hungary 2006-2014 all show these ingredients combining. When all four align during acute crises, extremist parties surge from 0.3-2.6% to 7-37% within years. Between 1920-1939, Europe's democracies fell from 24 to 11‚Äîa 54% collapse.
